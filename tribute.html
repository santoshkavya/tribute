Ratan Tata

1937-2024

"Leadership is about taking responsibility, not making excuses"

Biography

Ratan Tata was an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and investor who passed away on October 9, 2024 at the age of 86 Tata was the chairman of Tata Sons and Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, and again from 2016 to 2017. He joined the Tata Group in 1962, starting at the bottom on the shop floor of Tata Steel. He led the company's global expansion, acquiring Tetley, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus. Philanthropy Tata was a strong believer in corporate responsibility and ethical leadership. He directed 60–65% of Tata Sons' dividends to charitable causes. The Tata Trusts, which he guided, funded initiatives in healthcare, education, rural development, and other areas. Awards Tata received the Padma Bhushan in 2000, and the Padma Vibhushan in 2008. In 2018, he was invited to Buckingham Palace by Prince Charles to receive a Lifetime Achievement Award for his philanthropic efforts. Education Tata graduated from Cornell University with a degree in architecture and structural engineering. He also completed an advanced management program at Harvard Business School in 1975. Other achievements Tata was the first Indian to fly an F-16 Falcon in 2007. He was also a member of the board of the International Investment Council of South Africa, and a member of the Asia-Pacific Advisory Committee of the New York Stock Exchange.
